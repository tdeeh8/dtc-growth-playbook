# Channel Allocation & Budget Strategy

Last updated: 2026-04-09. Sources: Triple Whale (35k brands, BFCM 2025), Foxwell Digital (2026 State of Agencies), Brainlabs/Meta (17 incrementality studies), Common Thread Collective (PAM model), Stella (225 geo-tests), Northbeam (MMM+), Pilothouse, Omnisend, eMarketer, Digiday.

## Core Methodology (Evergreen)

**Don't use fixed percentage ratios.** Channel allocation should be driven by profitability data and incrementality signals, not templates. A brand selling $200 skincare has completely different channel economics than a $30 impulse product.

**Concentrate before diversifying.** Master one channel before adding a second. Splitting $5K/month across four channels means none get enough data to optimize. One channel at 3.5x+ ROAS for 60+ days before expanding.

**Channels are interdependent, not independent.** Cutting Meta TOF can increase Google CPA by 30% because Meta drives awareness that Google captures. Always model cross-channel effects before reallocating.

**Owned channels (Email/SMS) are structurally underallocated.** Email ROI: $36-42 per $1 spent. SMS ROI: $71 per $1. These are 10x better ROI than paid channels, yet most brands underinvest. Email/SMS should deliver 20-30%+ of total revenue.

## Current Playbook [Valid Q2 2026 — review July 2026]

### Channel Roles (2026)

| Channel | Primary Role | When to Increase | When to Decrease |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Meta** | Prospecting, demand generation, audience building | Creative testing shows strong hooks + proven MAT message | Frequency ceiling hit, CPMs rising without proportional ROAS, audience saturated |
| **Google Search** | Demand capture, high-intent conversion | Branded search volume growing (sign TOF is working) | CPCs rising faster than CVR, impression share already >90% |
| **Google Shopping/PMax** | Product discovery + conversion | Strong product feed, competitive pricing, AOV supports CPC | Low margin products where CPCs eat profit |
| **TikTok** | Awareness + direct commerce (TikTok Shop) | UGC/creator content available, target demo skews 18-34 | Low creator content capacity, product doesn't demo well on video |
| **Amazon** | Bottom-funnel capture | Organic rank near page 1, seasonal spikes, strong reviews | Poor organic rank + high ACOS = throwing money at a listing problem |
| **Email/SMS** | Retention, conversion of warm traffic, highest ROI | Always. Increase flow sophistication before campaign volume. | Never decrease — optimize instead |
| **YouTube/CTV** | Upper-funnel awareness + emerging direct conversion | Video assets exist, brand needs awareness, $33B+ CTV market | No video production capacity, low-AOV impulse products |
| **Pinterest** | Seasonal, visual/lifestyle categories | Visual product, gifting seasons, home/fashion/beauty | Non-visual products, B2B, low-design categories |

**Key 2026 shifts:**
- Meta still dominates social commerce (~52% share) but growth decelerated to 4.2% YoY. Diversification is no longer optional.
- TikTok Shop hit $15.8B US sales in 2025 (+108% YoY). No longer just an awareness play — direct commerce capability.
- Google Shopping taking budget share from Google Search. Best practice: 60% Shopping/PMax, 40% Search.
- CTV ad spend: $33B in 2025, projected $38B in 2026. Google launched shoppable CTV ads (Jan 2026) with QR codes.

### Allocation by Brand Stage (Canonical Source)

**Stage 1: New / Pre-Revenue ($0-$1M)**
- **100% on ONE channel** until 3.5x+ ROAS sustained for 60+ days
- Most DTC brands start with Meta (best prospecting) or Google Shopping (best unit economics)
- Don't diversify yet. Prove unit economics on one platform first.
- Email/SMS: Set up automated flows (welcome, abandoned cart, post-purchase) even before paid scales

**Stage 2: Growth ($1-10M, $3K+/month ad spend)**
- **Primary channel: 60-70%** (proven performer — usually Meta or Google)
- **Secondary channel: 20-30%** (whichever of Meta/Google you didn't start with)
- **Testing: 10%** (TikTok, Pinterest, or new creative formats)
- Email/SMS should be delivering 20%+ of revenue from flows at this stage

**Stage 3: Scaling ($10-50M)**
- **Meta: 40-50%** (prospecting + ASC)
- **Google: 25-35%** (Shopping/PMax + branded Search)
- **TikTok/YouTube: 10-15%** (if demo fits and creative capacity exists)
- **Email/SMS: target 25-30% of total revenue** (not spend — revenue attribution)
- Begin quarterly incrementality tests on largest channel

**Stage 4: Enterprise ($50M+)**
- Incrementality-driven allocation — no fixed ratios
- Run geo-holdout tests quarterly on each major channel
- Use MMM (Northbeam MMM+, Meta Robyn, or Sellforte) for strategic planning
- Allocate by iROAS tiers: scale highest incremental return, maintain moderate, cut lowest to defensive floor

### Cross-Channel Halo Effects

These are real but often overstated. Use them for planning but verify with incrementality tests:

- **Meta TOF → Google branded search:** Meta prospecting drives 19% more search visits (Brainlabs, 17 studies). BUT only 30% of those branded search conversions are truly incremental — 70% would happen organically.
- **Email engagement → Google ROAS:** Warm email recipients search branded terms. High email engagement correlates with higher branded search volume.
- **Meta/TikTok TOF → Email list growth:** Paid acquisition drives new subscribers. If paid creative is fatigued, email list growth slows even if Klaviyo is healthy.
- **Cutting one channel raises another's CPA:** Reducing Meta spend can increase Google CPA by 25-30% within 2 weeks because awareness pipeline dries up.

### When to Add a New Channel

**Prerequisites (all must be true):**
1. Primary channel at $10K+/month with stable positive ROAS
2. You've exhausted creative testing and audience expansion on current channels
3. One of these saturation signals: CAC climbing despite optimization, ROAS flattening with increased spend, frequency ceiling hit (Meta 3.5+/7d)

**Minimum viable test budgets for new channels:**
- TikTok: $1.5K-$3K over 2-4 weeks (need 50 conversions to exit learning)
- Google Shopping (if adding to Meta): $3K-$5K over 4 weeks
- Pinterest: Limited data — start with $1K-$2K seasonal test
- YouTube/CTV: $5K+ for meaningful reach data

**Don't add a channel if:**
- Below $3K/month total ad spend (focus on one platform)
- Your primary channel still has untapped scaling potential (creative, audiences, geos)
- You can't produce channel-native content (TikTok requires UGC; Pinterest requires visual assets)

### Budget Reallocation Triggers

| Signal | Threshold | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Audience reach saturation | 85%+ of available audience reached | Reduce 20-30% or shift objective |
| Frequency ceiling | 3.5+ prospecting / 6+ retargeting (7d) | Shift budget or refresh creative |
| Rising marginal CPA | Next spend increment costs 30%+ more per conversion | Shift 10-15% to higher-iROAS channel |
| Platform outperformance gap | One channel 25-30%+ better iROAS for 2+ weeks | Move 10-15% from underperformer |
| Impression share maxed (Google) | >90% impression share | Budget is ceiling — CPCs will rise, not conversions |
| MER declining despite stable platform ROAS | MER drops while individual channels look fine | Attribution inflation — run incrementality test |

**Reallocation rules:**
- Monthly rebalancing cadence (daily monitoring, monthly action)
- Maximum 10-20% budget shift per reallocation cycle
- Never cut a channel entirely without running a holdout test first — you may be killing a halo effect

### Seasonal Budget Planning

**Q4/BFCM Framework:**
- **August:** Begin planning. Lock creative production calendar. Set budget reserves.
- **September-October:** Ramp acquisition spend +30%. This is the best acquisition window — new customers acquired here become repeat buyers during BFCM.
- **Pre-BFCM (1-2 weeks before):** Launch sale early. Less competition, lower CPMs.
- **BFCM weekend:** Consider pacing down (auction is most expensive). Foxwell Digital recommends going big the week BEFORE Black Friday.
- **Cyber Monday:** Last push.
- **December:** Shift to gifting messaging. Retargeting heavy.

**Budget allocation:**
- ~40% of annual online sales occur Oct-Dec
- ~25% of annual media budget concentrated in BFCM 4-day window (if running a sale)
- High season (40% of year): 50-60% of annual budget
- Evergreen (40%): Steady-state, list building, flow optimization
- Low season (20%): Reduce paid, focus organic and retention

**Low-season strategy:** Don't go dark. Maintain brand presence at reduced spend. Focus on: email/SMS flow optimization, organic content, list building, creative production for next high season.

## Diagnostic Signals

- **Spending more but revenue is flat** → Check MER trend. If MER is declining, you're hitting diminishing returns on current channels. Run incrementality test before increasing budget.
- **Meta ROAS looks great but total business isn't growing** → Meta may be over-attributing. Check MER and blended ROAS (see measurement.md). Halo effect may be inflating platform numbers.
- **Added TikTok but Meta performance dropped** → Likely audience overlap or budget cannibalization. Check if TikTok is reaching the same demo Meta was. Consolidate targeting.
- **Email revenue is 5% of total** → Flows are broken or missing. Email should deliver 20-30% of revenue. Check welcome, abandoned cart, and post-purchase flows before increasing paid spend.
- **Google branded search volume declining** → TOF pipeline is drying up. Check Meta prospecting volume and creative health. Branded search is a lagging indicator of TOF effectiveness.
- **Q4 CPMs spiking, ROAS dropping** → Normal seasonal competition. Don't panic-cut. Lean on warm audiences built Sept-Oct. Shift messaging to urgency/offers.
