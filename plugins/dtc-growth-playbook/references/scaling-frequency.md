# Scaling and Frequency Management

## Frequency Thresholds by AOV

- Low AOV ($20-60): above 4-5 per 7 days = diminishing returns. Impulse buys. 5 views without buying = they won't.
- Mid AOV ($60-200): push to 5-7 per 7 days. Legitimate consideration phase.
- High AOV ($200+): frequency matters less (conversion is off-platform). 8+ per 7 days = wasted.

Real diagnostic: plot frequency against CTR. Find the inflection where frequency rises and CTR drops. That's "too high" for that specific account.

## Scaling When Hitting Frequency Ceiling

Audience saturated (frequency climbing, reach flattening):
1. New creative concepts (not variants) to open new audience clusters through Andromeda
2. New channels (TikTok, YouTube Shorts, CTV)
3. Influencer seeding for organic awareness
4. New geos

CPA climbing but reach still growing = creative fatigue, not saturation. 60% of pipeline should be net-new concepts.

Scaling sequence (budget is LAST):
1. Max out creative diversity
2. Add/increase Layer 1 reach
3. Add new channels
4. Add new geos
5. Then increase budget

## Big Audience but Narrow Delivery

Algorithm being lazy, not audience size problem. Causes:
- Creative too similar: Andromeda treats similar ads as one, serves to same cluster. Need genuinely different formats (polished video vs. raw UGC vs. meme vs. founder talking head).
- Conversion event too narrow: only a specific person type buys, Meta only finds that type. Run Layer 1 reach alongside purchase to force new impressions.
- CBO concentrating spend: dumps budget into cheapest-converting ad set. Try ABO or split campaigns.
- Pixel history biased: years of data on one customer type. New creative concepts retrain Andromeda fastest.
