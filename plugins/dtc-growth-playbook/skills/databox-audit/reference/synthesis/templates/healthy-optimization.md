# Template: Healthy / Optimization

**Trigger:** No pattern triggered with HIGH confidence; account scores ≥ Healthy on all framework dimensions.
**Precedence:** Tier 7 — lowest. Loses to all six other patterns. This template is the default when none of the diagnostic patterns hit. The report shifts from triage to upside — the question stops being "what's broken" and becomes "what's the next ceiling we can push through."

## Report Lead (one paragraph, 4 sentences max)

Template the synthesizer fills:

"Account is fundamentally healthy. {Specific positive numbers — MER at {X}x against breakeven of {Y}x, owned-channel share at {Z}%, TOF allocation in band, tracking variance under the threshold}. {Synthesis — what's working at the structural level, e.g., 'creative refresh cadence is steady, retention engine is funded, allocation matches stage'}. Here's what would unlock the next level — the testing roadmap, the retention deepening, and the channel expansion in priority order."

Example (literal sample, fill with realistic numbers):

"Account is fundamentally healthy. MER is 2.1x against a CM2-derived breakeven of 1.8x, owned-channel share is 24% (in the 20-30% Healthy band), TOF spend is 39% (mid-band for stage), and platform-vs-Shopify variance is under 18% on every channel. The structural pieces are in place — creative refresh has been monthly, lifecycle flows are live and updated within the last 90 days, and the channel mix matches the Growth-stage allocation guide. Here's what would unlock the next level — the testing roadmap, the retention deepening, and the channel expansion in priority order."

### Sub-Pattern Worked Example #2 — healthy fundamentals with one or two specific upside levers

Use this sub-flavor when the account is broadly healthy AND there are one or two clearly identifiable upside levers — an underexploited channel, a retention-deepening opportunity, an audience-expansion test that hasn't been run. The first sub-flavor is "everything's in band, what should we test?"; this one is "everything's in band AND there's a specific bet worth making." The body shifts from broad testing roadmap to a focused 1-2 lever recommendation with a clear hypothesis.

"Account is fundamentally healthy. MER is 2.4x against a 1.7x CM2-derived breakeven, owned share is 27% (top of the Healthy band), and platform variance is under 12% across the board. Two specific upside levers stand out from an otherwise in-band picture: (1) TikTok Shop hasn't been activated despite the brand fitting the Mass-AOV profile and 38% of organic traffic already coming from TikTok — a tested-but-not-funded channel; and (2) the post-purchase upsell flow doesn't exist (welcome + abandoned-cart + browse-abandon are live and Healthy, but no upsell sequence) — adding it could lift owned share another 2-4 percentage points without changing acquisition spend. Both are bets, not corrections — the sequence is to run a TikTok Shop pilot for 60 days and ship the upsell flow in parallel. Here's what would unlock the next level: the focused 2-lever plan, sequenced for measurable read-out within a quarter."

**Diagnosis sequence shifts (sub-pattern #2 vs #1):**
- Skip the broad testing roadmap framing; lead instead with the 1-2 named upside levers that surfaced in the diagnostic pass.
- For each lever, pre-build the bet structure (hypothesis, success threshold, decision date, kill criteria) so the operator can ship without an extra spec round.
- Sequence levers in priority of expected revenue lift × confidence — the highest-confidence small bet often beats the lower-confidence big bet for first sequencing.
- Reserve broad testing roadmap (sub-pattern #1) for the appendix as the secondary upside menu, not the primary lead.

## Money Page lead (Page 1)

The Money Page is the dollarized one-pager that opens the report. For Healthy / Optimization, the framing flips from triage to upside — "the account is fundamentally healthy; the recoverable opportunity is in deepening what works, not fixing what's broken."

**Headline template (one line, dollar-led):**

"~${X}/mo of optimization upside — account is healthy ({MER above breakeven, owned share in band, TOF in band, tracking clean}); the recoverable opportunity is in {specific area — incrementality on capture spend / retention deepening / channel pilot} where benchmarks suggest a {pct}% gain is realistic."

The dollar figure is upside potential, not loss recovery. Sizing math: (current monthly revenue from the targeted lever) × (modeled lift % from benchmark gap to Best-in-Class). Show the math underneath: "${X}/mo upside = {lever-area monthly revenue} × {modeled lift %}." Frame explicitly as a bet, not a fix — the diagnostic ruled out the other six patterns.

**The One Thing — pattern-specific framing:**

The One Thing is a named test or deepening bet — specify the highest-leverage lever (geo-holdout incrementality test on a capture channel, channel pilot for a tested-but-not-funded platform like TikTok Shop, or a retention-deepening flow like post-purchase upsell or win-back). Avoid "test more" or "look for upside" phrasings; the WHAT must name the test or flow, the channel/audience, the success threshold, the duration, and the readout date. The bet structure (hypothesis, kill criteria, decision rule) is part of the WHAT, not separate.

**5-day operator sequence (Mon–Fri shape):**

Day 1 = identify the highest-leverage upside lever from the diagnostic (rank by expected revenue lift × confidence × resource cost). Day 2 = write the test spec or build spec (control vs treatment, sample size for significance, duration, success threshold, kill criteria). Day 3 = brief the implementation owner and pre-register the decision rule (what we'll do at each outcome). Day 4 = launch the test or ship the first build into a 5-10% holdout group for clean lift measurement. Day 5 = stand up the readout calendar and confirm post-test decision rights with the account owner. The synthesizer fills the Mon–Fri grid from Action #1's HOW field.

**Money Page lead — failure modes (what NOT to write on Page 1):**

- Don't write the Money Page as if something is broken — the diagnostic ruled out all six other patterns, so framing the dollar figure as "loss" or "waste" misrepresents the account state and erodes credibility with a stakeholder who already knows the fundamentals are healthy.
- Don't size the dollar number as "lost revenue" — it's upside potential against a healthy baseline; frame it explicitly as a bet (with a readout date and kill criteria), not a fix.
- Don't recommend broad sweeping changes — Healthy mode is a "deepen what's working" mode. Recommending a portfolio-wide rebalance or full-funnel overhaul on a Healthy report invites over-correction and risks breaking what's currently in band.

## Funnel Health Section Adaptation

When this pattern is dominant, the Funnel Health section's lead paragraph reframes around the pattern. Specifically:
- Lead with: "Account is fundamentally healthy. The diagnostic dimensions are all in band — this section shifts from 'what's broken' to 'what's the next ceiling we can push.'"
- Charts: keep the standard funnel charts but reframe captions toward upside ("CTR at 2.1% — Healthy; ceiling is 2.8% with creative-quality lift"). Add an upside-mapping chart that overlays current performance against the next-tier benchmark.
- Body chart caption: "Each dimension scored vs benchmark band. Healthy across the board — opportunity is moving toward the upper edge of each band."

## Priority Actions Default Shape

The first 1-2 Priority Actions for this pattern follow a default shape (note: in Healthy mode, "Priority Actions" become "Priority Tests" or "Priority Investments" — the framing shifts because nothing is broken):

**Action #1 default — Run the highest-leverage incremental test.**

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **WHAT** | "Run a {named test — geo holdout / incrementality / new audience expansion / new creative format / channel-pilot} on {channel} for {N} days, against a {named control variant}. Document hypothesis, sample size, success threshold, and read-out date upfront." |
| **WHY** | "Account is healthy across diagnostics; the next-tier lift comes from learning what's actually incremental vs what would have happened anyway. Highest-leverage test is {X} because {reasoning — e.g., 'Meta retargeting is 14% of spend at 3.2x ROAS, but we don't know if email/SMS would have captured these conversions; a geo holdout would settle that and rightsize the budget.'}" |
| **HOW** | "1) Write the test spec: control vs treatment definition, geo/audience split, sample size to reach significance, duration, success threshold. 2) Brief the implementation owner on test mechanics. 3) Pre-register the hypothesis + decision rule (what we'll do at each outcome). 4) Run the test for the planned duration; resist the temptation to peek + stop early. 5) Read out at the planned date with explicit decision: scale, kill, or run again." |
| **WHEN** | "Spec written this week; test launched within 14 days; readout at end of {N}-day test window (typically 21-42 days depending on test type)." |
| **WHO** | "Agency strategist owns the spec + readout; agency PPC manager (or lifecycle owner if it's an email test) owns implementation; account owner approves any test that touches >10% of spend." |
| **EXPECTED IMPACT** | "Test outcomes inform a {$X-Y}/month allocation decision and feed the calibration loop for future audits. Direct lift expectation: 0-15% in the test window, depending on the incrementality finding (could also be 0%, which is a valuable null result). Confidence: HIGH that we get a usable read; LOW on which direction it'll go (that's the point). Escalation: if the test can't be cleanly run (e.g., insufficient volume for significance), pivot to a smaller-scope test or stack a sequence of micro-tests." |
| **MEASUREMENT** | "Day 7 post-launch: is the test running cleanly (control + treatment groups behaving as expected, no spillover)? Day {N} (readout date): does the result clear the pre-registered success threshold? Day {N+30}: was the post-test decision implemented and what did it move?" |

**Action #2 default — Deepen the retention layer past Healthy into Best-in-Class.**

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **WHAT** | "Move retention work from maintenance to expansion — ship segment-level personalization in the welcome/post-purchase flows, build the post-purchase upsell flow if it doesn't exist, build a win-back flow targeting 90+ day lapsed customers. Sequence in priority of expected revenue share lift." |
| **WHY** | "Owned share is in band ({X}%) but the upper edge of the band ({Y}%) is the next ceiling. Best-in-Class brands at this stage hit {Z}% from owned channels. Addresses Finding #2: the structural pieces are in place; the upside is in deepening, not rebuilding." |
| **HOW** | "1) Brief the lifecycle owner on the 3 priority builds (segment-level personalization, upsell flow, win-back flow) with category benchmarks for each. 2) Sequence over 60-90 days: build #1 ships in 30 days, build #2 in 60, build #3 in 90. 3) Each build gets a holdout group (5-10%) for clean lift measurement. 4) Benchmark each against current owned-channel RPR + the Klaviyo Best-in-Class band. 5) Roll learnings from earlier builds into later ones (e.g., personalization rules that worked in welcome get extended to upsell)." |
| **WHEN** | "First build (segment personalization) shipped within 30 days; full 3-build sequence complete in 90 days." |
| **WHO** | "Lifecycle / email manager owns build execution; agency strategist owns sequencing + benchmark sourcing; client design provides creative as needed." |
| **EXPECTED IMPACT** | "Each new flow typically adds 1-3 percentage points of total revenue share over 60-90 days; combined target is +5 percentage points of revenue from owned within 6 months (e.g., 24% → 29%). Confidence: MEDIUM on the magnitude (depends on list size and segment quality); HIGH that retention deepening at Healthy state moves the needle. Escalation: if any single build's holdout-group lift is <1 percentage point at 60 days, the constraint is segmentation logic not flow existence — escalate to a segmentation/data audit before continuing the sequence." |
| **MEASUREMENT** | "Day 30: is build #1 (segment personalization) shipped and showing ≥1 percentage point of revenue share lift in the holdout? Day 60: are builds #1 + #2 live, combined holdout-group lift ≥2 percentage points? Day 90: full 3-build sequence live; total owned-channel revenue share trending toward Best-in-Class band?" |

## Per-Channel Page Adaptation

How per-channel pages adjust under this pattern:
- Each per-channel page leads with "in band" status and shifts to upside framing: "{Platform} is performing in the Healthy band. Next-tier opportunity here is {specific lever — new audience, new creative format, expansion into a tested-but-not-funded ad type}."
- Replace "fix" recommendations with "test" or "expand" recommendations — explicitly frame as bets, not corrections.
- Add a "what would move this from Healthy to Best-in-Class" line per platform: the named lever and the expected lift.
- For platforms where the operator may want to pull back (capture-heavy, late-stage channels): note "in band — hold; risk of over-correcting a healthy channel is real."
- Where a sub-pattern #2 named lever applies to a specific channel (e.g., TikTok Shop activation, channel-specific upsell flow), surface the lever directly on that channel's page with the sized bet.

## Pattern-Specific Appendix Material

Required appendix sections under this pattern:
- **Healthy-State Scorecard** — each framework dimension with current value, Healthy band, Best-in-Class band, and the named lever to move from Healthy to Best-in-Class.
- **Testing Roadmap (90-day)** — 3-5 prioritized tests sequenced over the next quarter, each with hypothesis, design, sample size, expected lift, and decision date.
- **Channel Expansion Candidates** — channels not currently active that could fit the brand's stage and AOV profile (e.g., TikTok Shop, Connected TV, affiliate). Includes the readiness checklist before launching each.
- **Retention Deepening Roadmap** — segment-level personalization, post-purchase upsell, win-back, predictive churn flows. Sequenced 60-90 days with the dependency map.
- **Bet-Sizing Worksheet** — for sub-pattern #2 contexts: per-lever expected revenue lift × confidence × resource cost, ranked by score and sequenced for measurable readout within a quarter.
- **Healthy-State Defensibility Note** — explicit reasoning for why this account did NOT trigger any other pattern, with the diagnostic checks that ruled each one out — so a stakeholder asking "are we sure nothing's broken?" gets a documented answer.
