# Email and SMS Strategy

## Flows vs. Campaigns

Flows generate 41% of total email revenue from 5.3% of sends. RPR ~18x higher than campaigns. Prioritize flow optimization before campaign volume.

## Core Flows (Priority Order)

1. Welcome series (48% of flow revenue from new buyers)
2. Cart abandonment (highest recovery ROI)
3. Browse abandonment (30-35% open rates, 3-5% conversion)
4. Post-purchase / cross-sell (reduce returns, increase LTV)
5. Win-back (re-engage lapsed)

## Nurture Series — 60% Cohort Rule (Stroud)

Set nurture length to match time for 60% of cohort to convert. 60% convert in 15 days = nurture runs 21-30 days. High-ticket: 45-90 days.

Content: winning VP from MAT as backbone. Mix education, social proof, offers. SMS as reinforcement. Storytelling > pitch sequences.

## Seasonal Frequency — Segment by Engagement

- Engaged (opened/clicked last 30 days): up to 3x/day for short bursts (BFCM)
- Semi-engaged (60-90 days): 1x/day max during peak
- Disengaged (90+ days): exclude from high-frequency. Re-engagement flow instead.
- After high-frequency push: pause 3-5 days for recovery.

## AI Features

- AI product recommendations: 3.75% click rate lift average, 8.79% for top performers
- Predicted CLV segmentation: 15-25% higher RPR (only ~20% of accounts use this)
- Predictive send-time optimization
